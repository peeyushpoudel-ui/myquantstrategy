Setting up your strategy

You subscribed — here's how to get signals on your chart in under 5 minutes.

Add the indicator to your chart

Follow these steps after your subscription is confirmed.

1

Make sure your TradingView username is set

Go to your My Account page and make sure your TradingView username is saved. We use this to grant you access to the indicator. Without it, we can't add you.

2

Wait for access confirmation

After subscribing, we'll add your TradingView username to the indicator's access list. This usually happens within a few hours. You'll see the indicator appear in your TradingView account once access is granted.

3

Open TradingView and go to your chart

Go to tradingview.com and open a chart. Set the symbol to MNQ1! (Micro Nasdaq 100 Futures) and the timeframe to 1 minute.

4

Add the indicator

Click the Indicators button at the top of the chart (or press /). Go to the Invite-only scripts tab. You'll see the strategy listed there. Click it to add it to your chart.

5

You're live

The indicator is now on your chart. You'll see the daily bias label, the opening range box, entry signals, and stop loss / take profit lines. Everything updates automatically.

What you'll see on your chart

Here's what each element means — no guessing required.

🏷️

Daily status label

Appears at 9:45 AM ET. Shows LONG BIAS (green), SHORT BIAS (red), or NO TRADE TODAY (gray). Tells you instantly if today is a trading day.

🟡

Yellow box

The opening range from 9:30–9:45 AM ET. This is the range the strategy watches for breakouts.

🔺

Entry signals

Green triangle below a candle = long entry. Red triangle above a candle = short entry. When you see one, take the trade.

📏

Stop loss & take profit lines

Red line = stop loss. Green line = take profit. These appear automatically when a trade is active. Place your orders at these levels.

🔵

Blue EMA line

The 50-day moving average. Part of the strategy's internal logic — you don't need to do anything with it.

⚫

Win/loss dots

Green dot = trade hit take profit (win). Red dot = trade hit stop loss or closed at end of day (loss).

📊

Info table

Top right corner. Shows today's status, trading days (Tue–Fri), signal window (9:45–10:30 AM ET), and close time (3:58 PM ET).

⏐

10:30 cutoff line

Dashed white vertical line at 10:30 AM ET. No new signals fire after this time. If no signal by 10:30, you're done for the day.

Set up alerts (recommended)

Get notified on your phone so you don't have to watch the screen all morning.

1

With the indicator on your chart, click the Alert button (clock icon) at the top of TradingView, or press Alt+A.

2

Under Condition, select MNQ Opening Range from the dropdown.

3

Choose which alert you want. The most useful ones are Long Signal, Short Signal, and No Trade Today.

4

Under Notifications, check Push notification (requires TradingView mobile app) and/or Email.

5

Set Expiration to "Open-ended" so it doesn't stop working after a few days.

6

Click Create. Repeat for each alert type you want.

Available alerts

✓ Long Signal — notifies you when a long entry fires
✓ Short Signal — notifies you when a short entry fires
✓ Trade Closed — notifies you when a trade hits SL, TP, or closes at end of day
✓ No Trade Today — notifies you at 9:45 AM that today has no setup, so you can skip
✓ Long Bias Day — notifies you that today has a long bias, watch for entry
✓ Short Bias Day — notifies you that today has a short bias, watch for entry

Your daily routine

This is all you do, every trading day.

1

9:45 AM ET — Check the label

Open your chart or wait for the alert. If it says NO TRADE TODAY, you're done. If it says LONG BIAS or SHORT BIAS, stay at your chart.

2

9:45–10:30 AM ET — Wait for the signal

Watch for a green or red triangle. If one appears, enter the trade and place your stop loss and take profit at the red and green lines shown on your chart.

3

No signal by 10:30? Done.

If no triangle appears by the 10:30 cutoff line, walk away. No trades today.

4

Trade active? Let it run.

If you entered a trade, it will either hit take profit (green line), stop loss (red line), or close automatically at 3:58 PM ET. You don't need to manage it.

Common questions

I subscribed but don't see the indicator on TradingView↓
Access is granted manually and usually happens within a few hours. Make sure your TradingView username is correct on your My Account page. If it's been more than 24 hours, email myquantstrategy@gmail.com.
Do I need a paid TradingView plan?↓
No. A free TradingView account works. The only limitation is free accounts can have 2 indicators on a chart at the same time, so this indicator would use one of those slots.
Can I use this with any broker or prop firm?↓
Yes. The indicator shows signals on TradingView — you execute the trades on whatever platform your broker or prop firm uses (Tradovate, Rithmic, Project X, etc.).
What if I miss a signal?↓
Skip it. Never chase a late entry. There are typically 5-6 trades per month — missing one won't affect long-term results. Set up alerts so you don't miss the next one.
How many contracts should I trade?↓
That depends on your account size and risk tolerance. The backtest uses 5 MNQ contracts with a 25-point stop loss ($250 risk per trade). Scale up or down based on your account.
What happens when I cancel?↓
Your TradingView access is removed and the indicator stops appearing on your chart. You can re-subscribe anytime.
